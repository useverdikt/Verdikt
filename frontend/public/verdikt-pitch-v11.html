06 / 13
Override accountability

The approver can be human or policy.
The record is always there.

Named owner. Every below-threshold ship requires a named individual with title. Risk is owned by a person, not absorbed anonymously by the team.
Mandatory written justification. Scope of impact. Committed mitigations. Documented rationale — entered at the moment of decision, not reconstructed later.
Configurable override authority. You decide who can override — Tech Lead, Release Manager, QE Lead, VP Engineering, CTO. Configure approval authority to match your org. QE defines the standard; they are not the bottleneck.
Permanent, non-editable audit record. What was written at the moment of override remains on record. Changes are append-only and tamper-evident.
Release-ready accountability model. Model, prompt, and configuration changes move quickly, where ownership can blur. Verdikt records the approver as a named person or role with explicit justification.
Once an organisation requires named override accountability, they do not remove it. Removing it means publicly choosing to remove accountability.
